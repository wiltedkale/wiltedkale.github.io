February 2011—I'm sitting in the rec room at Utrecht University off-campus housing, laptop on a wood table between the lockers, playing Zerg. I thought I was going to go to school out here so I came early to look around, but with every minute of my free time I practiced. I was a Diamond Zerg with a 56% win rate but I was heading to Masters on a flaming chariot.

Last month I took a trip down memory lane, just to feel something again. I recalled everything that I had forgotten and relived the unforgettable things. I enjoyed time traveling so much that I picked a few moments to share.

#5: Dustin Browder wants StarCraft 2 to be easy

In a 2008 interview with starcraftedDOTorg, Dustin Browder defends StarCraft 2's interface changes for making the game easier to play and more approachable than its predecessor, StarCraft Brood War. Amusingly, modern RTS developers consider StarCraft 2 to be too difficult and execution-based for modern audiences. But in 2010 StarCraft 2 was seen as a bit too streamlined for hardened RTS experts. It makes one wonder the kind of simplicity and low skill requirement the next generation of RTS games will bring.


https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KWXRUk9ydEg&t=104s

#4: Battle Report 2

In Feburary 2010, Blizzard releases a series of 4 matches between the top StarCraft 2 developers, with commentary from Dustin Browder and Rob Simpson. Each match is unique and interesting and markets the game as a legitimate sequel to StarCraft. Hype level: over 9,000.

#1: Protoss gameplay reveal

An over 20-minute-long video showing off the Protoss units and their potential counters, one by one, against a rustic space tileset. It's a stunning first impression. There's a symbiosis between Dustin's passionate narration and the visuals displayed on screen. It quickly resonates that all of this isn't a prank: these guys had really made a sequel to StarCraft.
